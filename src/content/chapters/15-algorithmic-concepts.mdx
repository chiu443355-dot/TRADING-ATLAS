<p class="ch-intro">Algorithmic trading isn't just for hedge funds. Understanding these concepts makes you a sharper manual trader and opens the door to automating your edge entirely.</p>

<div class="card glass-gold">
  <div class="badge-pill bp-gold">Foundation</div>
  <h3>What Is a Trading Algorithm?</h3>
  <p style="margin-bottom:12px">A set of rules that automatically enters and exits trades based on predefined conditions. No emotion. No FOMO. No revenge trading. A well-coded strategy executes your system with machine-level consistency.</p>
  <p>Even if you never code an algo, understanding algorithmic concepts dramatically improves your manual trading by forcing you to define your strategy with mathematical precision.</p>
</div>

<div class="g2" style="margin-top:24px">
  <div class="card glass-green">
    <div class="badge-pill bp-green">Backtesting</div>
    <h3>Testing on Historical Data</h3>
    <p style="margin-bottom:10px">Running your strategy rules on past price data to see how it would have performed. The only way to know if you have a statistical edge before risking real money.</p>
    <p><strong style="color:#fff">Minimum sample:</strong> 200+ trades across multiple market conditions (trending, ranging, volatile). Never backtest on less than 3 years of data.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="card glass-red">
    <div class="badge-pill bp-red">Overfitting</div>
    <h3>The Backtester's Trap</h3>
    <p style="margin-bottom:10px">Optimizing your strategy parameters to perfectly fit past data. It looks incredible in backtesting and fails completely in live trading.</p>
    <p><strong style="color:#fff">The fix:</strong> Walk-forward analysis. Test on data you haven't seen. If performance degrades by more than 30%, you're overfitted.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div style="margin-top:28px">
  <div style="font-family:'Fira Code',monospace;font-size:10px;color:var(--gold);letter-spacing:.3em;margin-bottom:16px;">KEY ALGORITHMIC METRICS</div>
  <div class="pbg">
    <div class="pbc" style="border-top-color:var(--gold)"><h4>Sharpe Ratio</h4><p>Return per unit of risk. Above 1.0 is acceptable. Above 2.0 is excellent. Above 3.0 is world-class. Most manual traders operate at 0.3–0.8.</p></div>
    <div class="pbc" style="border-top-color:var(--red)"><h4>Maximum Drawdown</h4><p>The largest peak-to-trough decline in equity. If your system has a 40% max drawdown, you need to sustain 40% account loss during its worst period. Is your psychology ready for that?</p></div>
    <div class="pbc" style="border-top-color:var(--blue)"><h4>Win Rate vs Expectancy</h4><p>Win rate alone is meaningless. A 30% win rate with 1:4 R:R beats a 70% win rate with 1:0.5 R:R. Expectancy = (Win% × Avg Win) - (Loss% × Avg Loss).</p></div>
    <div class="pbc" style="border-top-color:var(--green)"><h4>Calmar Ratio</h4><p>Annual return ÷ Maximum Drawdown. Measures return relative to worst-case scenario. Professional funds target Calmar Ratio above 1.0.</p></div>
    <div class="pbc" style="border-top-color:var(--purple)"><h4>Recovery Factor</h4><p>Net profit ÷ Maximum Drawdown. Shows how much profit the system generates per unit of risk experienced. Above 3.0 is strong.</p></div>
    <div class="pbc" style="border-top-color:var(--teal)"><h4>Profit Factor</h4><p>Gross profit ÷ Gross loss. Above 1.5 is acceptable. Above 2.0 is good. The higher, the more robust the edge.</p></div>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="card glass-gold" style="margin-top:24px">
  <h3>Building Your First Simple System — Step by Step</h3>
  <div class="tl" style="margin-top:16px">
    <div class="tl-item"><h4>Define entry conditions precisely</h4><p>"Price breaks above 20-period EMA on 1H chart with RSI above 50 and previous candle is bullish engulfing at a 4H support zone." Every word must be specific and testable.</p></div>
    <div class="tl-item"><h4>Define exit conditions</h4><p>Stop loss: 10 pips below entry candle low. Take profit: 20 pips (1:2 R:R). Time-based exit: close all positions by 4PM IST.</p></div>
    <div class="tl-item"><h4>Code or manually backtest</h4><p>For manual backtesting: open TradingView, go back 2 years, scroll forward candle-by-candle, record every trade that meets your criteria.</p></div>
    <div class="tl-item"><h4>Analyze the data</h4><p>Calculate: win rate, average win, average loss, profit factor, max drawdown, expectancy. Does this system have a positive edge over 200+ trades?</p></div>
    <div class="tl-item"><h4>Forward test on demo</h4><p>Run live on demo for 3 months before any real capital. Does performance match backtesting within ±30%? If yes, go live with minimum position size.</p></div>
  </div>
</div>
