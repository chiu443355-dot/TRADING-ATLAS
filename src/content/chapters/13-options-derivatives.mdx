<p class="ch-intro">Options are the most powerful financial instruments available to retail traders — and the most misunderstood. Master these and you control risk like an institution.</p>

<div class="highlight-box">
  <h4>Why Options Matter</h4>
  <p>An option gives you the RIGHT (not obligation) to buy or sell an asset at a specific price before a specific date. Unlike spot trading, your maximum loss is capped at the premium you pay. This changes everything about risk management.</p>
</div>

<div class="g2">
  <div class="card glass-green">
    <div class="badge-pill bp-green">CALL Option</div>
    <h3>The Right to Buy</h3>
    <p style="margin-bottom:10px">A CALL gives you the right to buy an asset at the strike price. You profit when the asset rises above your strike + premium paid.</p>
    <p><strong style="color:#fff">Example:</strong> Buy a Nifty 22000 Call for ₹150. Nifty goes to 22500. Your option is worth ₹500. Profit: ₹350 per unit.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="card glass-red">
    <div class="badge-pill bp-red">PUT Option</div>
    <h3>The Right to Sell</h3>
    <p style="margin-bottom:10px">A PUT gives you the right to sell an asset at the strike price. You profit when the asset falls below your strike − premium paid.</p>
    <p><strong style="color:#fff">Example:</strong> Buy a Nifty 22000 Put for ₹120. Nifty drops to 21500. Your option is worth ₹500. Profit: ₹380 per unit.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div style="margin-top:28px">
  <div style="font-family:'Fira Code',monospace;font-size:10px;color:var(--gold);letter-spacing:.3em;margin-bottom:16px;">THE GREEKS — HOW OPTIONS MOVE</div>
  <div class="pbg">
    <div class="pbc" style="border-top-color:var(--gold)">
      <h4>Delta (Δ)</h4>
      <p>How much the option price moves for every ₹1 move in the underlying. Delta 0.5 = option moves ₹0.50 per ₹1 move. Deep ITM options have Delta ~1. OTM options have Delta near 0.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="pbc" style="border-top-color:var(--blue)">
      <h4>Gamma (Γ)</h4>
      <p>Rate of change of Delta. High Gamma = Delta changes quickly as price moves. ATM options have highest Gamma. Critical near expiry.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="pbc" style="border-top-color:var(--red)">
      <h4>Theta (Θ)</h4>
      <p>Time decay. Every day that passes, your option loses value (all else equal). Theta works against option buyers and FOR option sellers. Never hold options through weekends if avoidable.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="pbc" style="border-top-color:var(--green)">
      <h4>Vega (ν)</h4>
      <p>Sensitivity to implied volatility (IV). When IV rises, options become more expensive. Buy options before expected volatility events. Sell options when IV is high and likely to fall.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="pbc" style="border-top-color:var(--purple)">
      <h4>Rho (ρ)</h4>
      <p>Sensitivity to interest rates. Least important for short-term options. Affects LEAPS (long-dated options) significantly.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="pbc" style="border-top-color:var(--teal)">
      <h4>IV Rank / IV Percentile</h4>
      <p>Measures how current implied volatility compares to its historical range. IV Rank 80+ = sell premium. IV Rank 20− = buy premium. The most important metric for options strategy selection.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="card glass-gold" style="margin-top:24px">
  <h3>Core Options Strategies</h3>
  <div class="tl" style="margin-top:16px">
    <div class="tl-item"><h4>Long Call / Long Put</h4><p>Directional speculation with defined risk. Max loss = premium paid. Best when you expect a big move and IV is low.</p></div>
    <div class="tl-item"><h4>Covered Call</h4><p>Own the underlying, sell a call against it. Generates income (premium) but caps your upside. Ideal in sideways-to-mildly-bullish markets.</p></div>
    <div class="tl-item"><h4>Bull Call Spread</h4><p>Buy a lower strike call, sell a higher strike call. Reduces premium cost, caps both profit and loss. Better risk-reward than naked calls.</p></div>
    <div class="tl-item"><h4>Iron Condor</h4><p>Sell an OTM call spread + OTM put spread simultaneously. Profits when the underlying stays in a range. Favourite strategy of professional income traders.</p></div>
    <div class="tl-item"><h4>Straddle</h4><p>Buy both a call and put at the same strike. Profits from large moves in either direction. Best before earnings or major events when IV is still low.</p></div>
  </div>
</div>
