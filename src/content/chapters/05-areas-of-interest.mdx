<p class="ch-intro">Not all price levels are equal. Support and resistance zones are where the market repeatedly makes decisions. These are your entry zones.</p>
<div class="card glass-gold">
  <div class="badge-pill bp-gold">Core Rule</div>
  <h3>What Makes a Valid AOI?</h3>
  <p style="margin-bottom:12px">A price zone where the market has reacted <strong style="color:#fff">at least 3 times</strong>. Each reaction adds validity.</p>
  <p>AOIs must be <strong style="color:#fff">5–60 pips wide</strong>. Too narrow = missed entries. Too wide = bad risk-reward.</p>
</div>
<div class="g2">
  <div class="card glass-green"><div class="badge-pill bp-green">Support Zone</div><h3>Price Floor</h3><p>Selling pressure is met by enough buying to push price back up. Broken resistance often becomes new support — "flip zones."</p></div>
  <div class="card glass-red"><div class="badge-pill bp-red">Resistance Zone</div><h3>Price Ceiling</h3><p>Buying pressure met by enough selling to push price back down. Flip zones work both ways.</p></div>
</div>
<div class="warn-box">
  <h4>Common Mistake</h4>
  <p>Many traders enter ON THE BREAK — before the retest. Wait for the retest. Yes, you'll miss some trades. But the ones you take will be much higher quality.</p>
</div>
