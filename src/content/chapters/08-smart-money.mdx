<p class="ch-intro">Institutions don't trade like retail traders. They manipulate price to hunt your stop losses before making the real move. Understanding this changes everything.</p>
<div class="highlight-box">
  <h4>The Fundamental Truth</h4>
  <p>Institutional traders (banks, hedge funds, central banks) trade in billions. They NEED liquidity to fill massive orders. Liquidity sits at YOUR stop losses. They engineer moves to collect that liquidity before reversing.</p>
</div>
<div class="card glass-gold" style="margin-top:24px">
  <h3>What is an Order Block?</h3>
  <p style="margin-bottom:14px">The last candle(s) before a significant institutional move. Represents where institutions placed large orders. When price returns to this zone, institutions often defend their position — creating a bounce.</p>
  <p>Think of it as: the price level where a bank said "we want to buy a billion dollars here." When price comes back, they buy more.</p>
</div>
<div class="pbg">
  <div class="pbc" style="border-top-color:var(--gold)"><h4>Normal Order Block</h4><p>Last bearish candle before a major bullish move. Or last bullish candle before a major bearish move.</p></div>
  <div class="pbc" style="border-top-color:var(--blue)"><h4>Mitigation Block</h4><p>A failed order block that price has already visited. Orders are filled — "mitigated."</p></div>
  <div class="pbc" style="border-top-color:var(--purple)"><h4>Breaker Block</h4><p>After structure breaks, the old order block becomes a breaker — now acts as resistance instead of support.</p></div>
  <div class="pbc" style="border-top-color:var(--teal)"><h4>Fair Value Gap (FVG)</h4><p>Price moves so fast it creates a gap in trading. Acts like a magnet — price returns to fill it before continuing.</p></div>
  <div class="pbc" style="border-top-color:var(--red)"><h4>Liquidity Hunt</h4><p>Institutions engineer brief moves to obvious stop-loss levels before reversing sharply. Buffer your stops by 5–10 pips.</p></div>
  <div class="pbc" style="border-top-color:var(--green)"><h4>Liquidity Sweep Reversal</h4><p>Price sweeps below key support (collecting stops) then immediately reverses above. Enter exactly where smart money enters.</p></div>
</div>
