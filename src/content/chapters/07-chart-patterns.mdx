<p class="ch-intro">Patterns are footprints of institutional behaviour. They repeat because human psychology and institutional mechanics don't change.</p>
<div class="card glass-gold">
  <div class="badge-pill bp-gold">Reversal Pattern</div>
  <h3>Head and Shoulders — The King of Reversals</h3>
  <div class="tl" style="margin-top:16px">
    <div class="tl-item"><h4>Left Shoulder</h4><p>Price rises, makes a high, then pulls back. Normal swing in uptrend.</p></div>
    <div class="tl-item"><h4>Head</h4><p>Price rises to a higher high (tallest peak), then pulls back to similar level.</p></div>
    <div class="tl-item"><h4>Right Shoulder</h4><p>Price attempts another rally but fails to match the head. Lower high = weakness.</p></div>
    <div class="tl-item"><h4>Neckline Break + Retest</h4><p>Break below + retest from below → SELL entry. Stop above right shoulder.</p></div>
  </div>
</div>
<div class="g2" style="margin-top:20px">
  <div class="card"><div class="badge-pill bp-green">Continuation</div><h3>Flags and Pennants</h3><p>After a strong move ("flagpole"), price consolidates. A pause, not a reversal. Entry: Breakout of flag in direction of original trend.</p></div>
  <div class="card glass-purple"><div class="badge-pill bp-purple">Reversal</div><h3>Double Top / Double Bottom</h3><p><strong style="color:#fff">Double Top (M):</strong> Two equal highs, second fails → bears gaining. <strong style="color:#fff">Double Bottom (W):</strong> Two equal lows, second holds → bulls gaining.</p></div>
</div>
<div class="highlight-box">
  <h4>Pattern + Context = Edge</h4>
  <p>A pattern alone is noise. A Head & Shoulders at a 4H resistance zone after a CHoCH, with a bearish engulfing on the right shoulder — that is a trade.</p>
</div>
