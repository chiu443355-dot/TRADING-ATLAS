<p class="ch-intro">Every pip that moves in forex, every tick in gold, every point on the Nifty — ultimately traces back to macro forces. Understanding them gives you an edge no indicator can provide.</p>

<div class="highlight-box">
  <h4>The Macro Hierarchy</h4>
  <p>Interest rates → Currency strength → Capital flows → Asset prices. Everything in global markets ultimately connects to this chain. Central banks control interest rates. Interest rates control currency values. Currency values drive trade and investment flows.</p>
</div>

<div class="g2" style="margin-top:24px">
  <div class="card glass-gold">
    <div class="badge-pill bp-gold">Central Banks</div>
    <h3>The Most Powerful Market Movers</h3>
    <p style="margin-bottom:10px">Central banks set interest rates, which determine the cost of borrowing and the return on savings. Higher rates = stronger currency (attracts capital). Lower rates = weaker currency.</p>
    <p><strong style="color:#fff">Key central banks:</strong> Fed (USD) · ECB (EUR) · RBI (INR) · Bank of Japan (JPY) · Bank of England (GBP)</p>
  </div>
  <div class="card glass-blue">
    <div class="badge-pill bp-blue">Interest Rate Cycles</div>
    <h3>The 4-Phase Economic Cycle</h3>
    <div class="tl" style="margin-top:12px">
      <div class="tl-item"><h4>Expansion</h4><p>Economy growing, employment rising. Central banks begin raising rates. Currency strengthens. Equities bull market.</p></div>
      <div class="tl-item"><h4>Peak</h4><p>Rates at highs. Growth slowing. Inflation still elevated. Risk assets start correcting.</p></div>
      <div class="tl-item"><h4>Contraction</h4><p>Central banks cut rates. Currency weakens. Gold and bonds rally as safe havens.</p></div>
      <div class="tl-item"><h4>Trough</h4><p>Lowest rates, economy bottoming. Best time to buy equities and growth assets before next expansion.</p></div>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="card glass-purple" style="margin-top:24px">
  <div class="badge-pill bp-purple">Advanced</div>
  <h3>Geopolitical Risk and Safe Havens</h3>
  <p style="margin-bottom:12px">When geopolitical tensions rise (wars, sanctions, elections), capital flows to <strong style="color:#fff">safe haven assets</strong>: USD, JPY, CHF, and Gold.</p>
  <p style="margin-bottom:12px">This creates predictable patterns: Middle East tensions → gold rally + oil spike. US-China trade disputes → USD strength + CNY weakness. Indian election uncertainty → INR volatility + FII outflows.</p>
  <p><strong style="color:#fff">Trading geopolitical risk:</strong> Identify the safe haven flows first. Don't fight them. Trade WITH the macro current, not against it. A perfect technical setup against a macro headwind fails far more often than a mediocre setup with macro tailwind.</p>
</div>

<div class="highlight-box">
  <h4>The Macro Trader's Edge</h4>
  <p>You don't need to predict geopolitics. You need to recognize when the macro environment is changing and position accordingly — weeks or months before most retail traders react. Read central bank statements. Watch rate differentials. Follow the smart money through Commitment of Traders (COT) reports.</p>
</div>
